            <h4> Computational Mathematics Research Group UGM </h4>
            Computational mathematics applies numerical methods to PDEs and inverse problems in science, engineering, and medical imaging. We focus on two areas:
            <ul>
                <li>
                Computational PDEs — solving real-world problems governed by partial differential equations using methods such as Finite Difference, Finite Volume, Finite Element, and Boundary Element. Applications include heat conduction, pollutant dispersion, and diffusion–convection in layered materials.
                </li>
                <li>
                Optimization methods — reconstructing images from incomplete data, with applications in tomography and industrial imaging. Current research explores iterative PDE-constrained optimization and multi-resolution tools like wavelets and shearlets.
                </li>
            </ul>
            <strong>Keywords</strong>. Finite Difference Method, Finite Volume Method, Finite Element Methods, Boundary Element Methods, Machine Learning, Heat Conduction, Pollutant Spread, Diffusion-Convection, Layered Materials, Inverse Problems, Bayesian inversion, Computed tomography, Computational inverse problems, Gaussian processes, Kalman filter, Wavelets, Shearlets, SBP-SAT Operator, High Order methods, Fluid dynamics
